## Dense encoder truncation exposure

The encoder silently truncates inputs at its maximum sequence length (wordpieces, specials included), so for chunks past that window the dense retriever scores a prefix. Any dense result on configs with high truncation exposure measures prefix retrieval, not whole-chunk retrieval — that is the deployment reality of pairing large chunks with a small encoder, and the exposure column quantifies how much of the grid it affects.
